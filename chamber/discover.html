Population

The Mexican capital has over 9 million inhabitants and more than 21 million in its metropolitan area, making it one of the most populous cities in the world (2021).

Tourism

Mexico City is a highly touristic destination, attracting millions of visitors each year. In 2019, the city welcomed over 14 million international tourists, generating an estimated revenue of $3.6 billion USD. With its rich cultural heritage, iconic landmarks like the Palacio de Bellas Artes and the ancient ruins of Teotihuacan, and vibrant culinary scene, Mexico City offers a diverse and immersive experience for travelers from around the world.

Food

Mexico City boasts an incredible variety of culinary delights. From street food stalls offering mouthwatering tacos and tamales to upscale restaurants serving gourmet Mexican cuisine, the city caters to all tastes. With regional specialties like mole, chiles en nogada, and street snacks like elote and churros, food lovers can indulge in a gastronomic adventure like no other.

Historical places

The architecture in Mexico City holds great significance, supported by impressive statistical data. The city boasts over 1,500 buildings classified as historical and cultural heritage sites. Additionally, there are estimated to be more than 6,000 constructions declared as historical monuments. These figures demonstrate the value placed on the preservation and appreciation of architecture in Mexico City.
